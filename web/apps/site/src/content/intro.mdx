# Teslasynth

Teslasynth is open-source MIDI synthesizer firmware for ESP32 that turns
interruptible high-voltage devices into musical instruments. Connect a Tesla
coil, flyback transformer, high-power laser, or any device that makes noise
when switched, and play it over MIDI.

The firmware drives up to 4 independent output channels through the ESP32's RMT
peripheral, producing precisely timed microsecond pulses. Each channel can play
up to 4 simultaneous notes from a library of 28 instruments and a set of
percussion presets.

## Features

- **Up to 4 output channels**, each independently configurable
- **28 instruments** — leads, pads, bass, brass, strings, and effects
- **Percussion presets** — full MIDI channel 10 support with burst and noise
  synthesis (to my knowledge, unique among interrupter firmware)
- **USB MIDI** (ESP32-S2 and S3) and **Bluetooth MIDI** (ESP32 only — see
  [MIDI setup](/midi))
- **Web installer** — flash firmware from your browser, no software needed
- **Web dashboard** — configure the device over Wi-Fi from any browser,
  including your phone at showtime
- **Python library** — render MIDI files offline, visualise signals, and
  analyse output before connecting any hardware

## Design

Teslasynth was built around a few principles:

**Accessible.** An ESP32 costs a few dollars and is available worldwide. The
firmware runs on any dev board with no custom hardware. There is no proprietary
software, no account, no licence fee. The firmware is GPL v3; the Python
library is LGPL v3.

**Easy to use.** Firmware installs from a browser with one click. Configuration
happens over Wi-Fi from any phone — no laptop, no serial cable. A new device
is ready to play in minutes.

**Safe.** Configurable duty cycle limits, pulse-width caps, and dead-time
enforcement protect your hardware at the firmware level. The Python library lets
you simulate and visualise output before connecting anything high-voltage. The
firmware is tested and the synthesis engine is independently testable offline.

**Standard MIDI.** Teslasynth is a transparent MIDI device. Any software or
hardware that sends standard MIDI works with it — no custom drivers, no special
plugins, no proprietary protocol.

**Modern.** Web-installable, web-configurable, open-source. The goal is to make
quality interrupter firmware so accessible and complete that there is no reason
to reach for anything else.

## Supported hardware

| Chip     | USB MIDI | Bluetooth MIDI |
|----------|----------|----------------|
| ESP32    | —        | ✓              |
| ESP32-S2 | ✓        | —              |
| ESP32-S3 | ✓        | —¹             |

¹ The ESP32-S3 supports Bluetooth in hardware, but this firmware builds for
USB only on S3. USB is the recommended connection for any serious coil
ensemble — Bluetooth MIDI introduces jitter on fast note runs and is
unreliable in the presence of strong electric fields.

Any development board based on these chips works. The firmware has been tested
on standard 4 MB flash modules.

## ⚠ Safety

**Teslasynth is designed to control high-voltage and high-power devices. These
are dangerous. Read this before connecting anything.**

- Tesla coils and flyback transformers produce lethal voltages. Never operate
  them unenclosed or without proper safety barriers.
- High-power lasers can cause permanent eye damage and fire. Use appropriate
  enclosures, interlocks, and eye protection.
- The firmware enforces configurable duty cycle and pulse-width limits to
  protect your hardware, but these are not a substitute for proper system-level
  safety design.
- Never work on live high-voltage circuits. Always discharge capacitors before
  touching any part of the circuit.

The firmware has no awareness of what it is connected to. Safe operation is
entirely the responsibility of the builder and operator.
